Project Update

VEX U: Over Under

At the start of the season, with so many new members the club was abundant with fresh ideas for this year's VEXU: "Over Under" competition. October saw this early brainstorming hit the drawing board, and many tasks are being worked on simultaneously:

1. The 15" drivetrain was completed over the summer, so work on its next upgrades began quickly. The mech team equipped it with a new catapult design and intake combo for fast-action scoring across the field.

2. Part of the collegiate-level competition has university teams design and build two robots. Our mechanical team finished the CAD of the 18" drivetrain, our second robot. Not only do the two robots differ in size, but they are driven differently as well. The 15" bot has 6 motors for 8 omni-wheels, giving it speed and quick maneuvering. The 18" was built as pictured below, scoring a 6-motor, 6-wheel direct-drive drivetrain with larger diameter wheels. It will still be equipped with an intake-catapult system, but additional modifications are being considered for greater functionality.

3. As pathfinding and odometry teams continue implementation, the programming team has begun working on PID control to ensure the robot moves as intended. Testing for the algorithm is benchmarked on a Python Pygame window, where a moving sprite simulates the expected error from the robot for the PID to respond to.

Community Outreach

10/3 Open House

We welcomed over 30 people to our Open House during startup week. We had several families show up which was awesome! We toured everyone around the lab, let them drive our bots, and showed them our 3D printer!

10/28 Haunted Halloween Booth

A couple of days ago we hosted a booth at UAA's Haunted Halloween Night! It was full of fun, candy, and robots! Around 200 youth and adults stopped by our booth!
