VEX U: Over Under Update
At the start of the season, with so many new members the club was overridden with fresh ideas for this year's VEXU: "Over Under" competition. However, October saw this early brainstorming hit the drawing board, and many tasks are being worked on simultaneously:
1. The 15" drivetrain was completed over the summer, so work on its next upgrades began quickly. The mech team equipped it with a new catapult design and intake compliment for fast-action scoring across the field.
2. Part of the collegiate-level competition has university teams design and build two robots. Our mechanical team finished our CAD of the 18" drivetrain, our second robot. Not only do two robots differ in size, but they are driven differently as well. The 15" spreads its six-motor power here

So far for Over Under we created a one out of our two drive trains with 6 motors and 8 wheels. This allows us to traverse the field with speed and accuracy. The second drivetrain has been made with CAD and is the review phase. We are currently working on prototyping the intake and tribal shooter with pneumatics and other mechanisms

On the programming side, we have completed drivetrain controls and are working on autonomous navigation with odometry and A* pathing. We are also working on sending data between the VEX Brain (i.e. computer) and a Raspberry Pi so we can take advantage of additional sensor and processor capacity.
