Team Spotlight
18" Drivetrain!
The mechanical team has been hard at work putting ideas into action this past month. The 15" drivetrain has received a few upgrades, including a catapult mechanism and an innovative intake design. Pictured here is our new robust 18" drivetrain, with six direct-drive motors and a custom water-jetted fender!
 
Rookie Challenges
To get our new members involved in our projects and familiar with the skills they’ll need, we developed “Rookie Challenges,” a set of challenges meant to push new members into exploring the various areas of our projects. They spanned skills such as basic motor programming, simple CAD for 3D printing, and using tools to build a catapult.
VEX U: Over Under Update
At the start of the season, with so many new members the club was overridden with fresh ideas for this year's VEXU: "Over Under" competition. However, October saw this early brainstorming hit the drawing board, and many tasks are being worked on simultaneously:

1)   The 15" drivetrain was completed over the summer, so work on its next upgrades began quickly. The mech team equipped it with a new catapult design and intake compliment for fast-action scoring across the field.

2)   Part of the collegiate-level competition has university teams design and build two robots. Our mechanical team finished our CAD of the 18" drivetrain, our second robot. Not only do two robots differ in size, but they are driven differently as well. The 15" bot has 6 motors for 8 omni-wheels, giving it speed and quick maneuvering. The 18" was built differently, scoring a 6-motor, 6-wheel direct-drive drivetrain with larger diameter wheels. It will still be equipped with an intake-catapult system, but additional modifications are being considered for greater functionality.

3)   As pathfinding and odometry teams continue implementation, the programming team has begun working on PID control to ensure the robot moves as intended. Testing for the algorithm is benchmarked on a Python Pygame window, where a moving sprite simulates the expected error from the robot for the PID to respond to.
