Project Update

VEX U: Over Under

Both VEX U bots came together during winter break and were officially named. Scrat is the smaller bot and Scratette is the larger bot, named after the squirrels from the Ice Age.

First Competition. On 1/20 we competed in the Alaska Remote Skills Competition, which we hosted ourselves. The goal for the teams who competed was to score as many points as possible during a 1-minute driver controlled period and 1-minute autonomous period. We scored 62 driver points and 47 autonomous points, winning us the competition.

Lessons Learned. Although we did well for our first competition, we still learned that we have a lot to work on before our next competition (Purdue Sigbot Slam and Jam on 2/24)! For one, we're redesigning our catapult on Scrat to be more reliable and shoot the triballs in a higher arc. We're adding rotational sensors to both catapults to improve reliability as well. We also learned that speed and accuracy in autonomous is crucial, so we'll be working on improving that with PIDs galore. Wish us luck!

Community Outreach
1/26 Girl Scouts Robotics Badge
We helped a local 4th grade Girl Scouts Troop earn their Robotics Badge by making a Remote Control Car! The scouts learned how to used a wiring diagam, upload codee to their ESP32 microcontroller, and use TinkerCAD to add designs to their car. They had a blast!
