CoffeeBot

Now that our VEX competition season is done, our CoffeeBot project, started last year, is moving into center stage. The parts for our first iteration robot have been taking a while to ship, but our programming team has not skipped a beat. Several important aspects of operating the bot are being worked on simultaneously. That includes,
  • Communicating with Arduinos through serial.
    The robot will consist of a "brain", a raspberry pi that controlls overall robot operations, and a number of tiny Arduinos, microcomputers that will control each motor. The brain will communicate with these microcomputers through a USB cable, so the team has been working on streamlining this communication through a serial connection.
  • Controlling the robot
    The auto-serving portion of the robot's programming will have to wait for the fundamentals to be made first. To help with testing, the programming team is working on getting a game controller functioning to control the robot's motions for now.
  • Inverse Kinematics
    The robot will have a number of joints, all that works together to position the hand at a target location. Ideally, we can tell the robot where to put the hand in 3D space, and it can calculate what angles to position its joints at that achieves this. This is where inverse kinematics come into play. We started this last year, and are finishing it up this semester!
  • Simulation!
    Although the hardware for the robot is on hold, it can't hold testing back! To accommodate the long shipping times, the team has already whipped up a 3D simulation of the arm that has already proven our Inverse Kinematics.
As for the arm itself, we are opting for an open source design that will enable us to quickly develop the codebase and build upon other people's ideas.
