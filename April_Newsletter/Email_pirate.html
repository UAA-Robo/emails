CoffeeBot

Now that our VEX competition season be done, our CoffeeBot project, started last year, be movin' onto main deck. The parts for our first iteration robot have been takin' blimey long time to ship, but me programmin' crew 'as not stopped their jig. Many important aspects o' operatin' the bot are hauling wind simultaneously. That includes,
  • Communicating with Arduinos through serial.
    The robot will consist of a "brain", a raspberry pi that controlls overall robot operations, and a number of tiny Arduinos, microcomputers that be controllin' each motor. The brain will communicate with these microcomputers through a USB cable, so the crew 'as been heavin' ho on streamlinin' this communication through a serial connection.
  • Controlling the robot
    The auto-servin' portion of the robot's programmin' must sit in port for the fundamentals to be made first. To help with testin', the programmin' crew is working on gettin' a game controller functionin' to control the robot's motions for now.
  • Inverse Kinematics
    The robot will have a number of joints, all that work together to position the hand at a target location. Ideally, me crew can tell the robot where to put the hand in 3D space, and it can calculate what angles to position its joints at that achieves this. This be where inverse kinematics come into play. We started this last year, and are finishin' it up this semester!
  • Simulation!
    Although the hardware for the robot be stuck at sea, it can't hold testing back! To accommodate the long shipping times, the crew 'as already whipped up a 3D simulation of the arm that 'as already proven our Inverse Kinematics.
