Project Update

VEX U: Over Under

November has been busy for the club's VEX U Project! Both the Mechanical and Programming teams are hard at work making the robots competition-ready. This past month, the mechanical side has continued to upgrade the bots with more components, and the programming team is making strides in accurate autonomous driving and operation.

Mechanical Team
The robots have rapidly taken shape in the past few weeks. The 15" robot was equipped with the intake system that was built last month, and the matching catapult is undergoing final tweaks. This robot will receive a "snowplow," retractable wings that will be used to push game objects around the field in the competition. Due to size requirements, a new design is being implemented, where the pneumatics will be mounted vertically rather than horizontally.

Programming Team
On the programming side, autonomous driving is the principal focus of this upcoming month. November saw the development of a virtual map. This is so we can tell the robot to drive to a "feature," like a loading zone, and the robot knows how to travel there internally. The map is made up of objects representing each feature, and calling any object returns its position to our pathing functions. The first pictures below show the results of a machine vision system we want to implement in the robots this year. Part of the competition we will be competing in involves autonomously picking up the green "triballs." during the match, these will be thrown everywhere, so the initial positions they occupy programmed into our virtual map are not sufficient in finding them on the field. Here, a camera and a raspberry pi are identifying the objects on the field. This will be hugely important during a match so that we can effectively find and score the triballs.

CoffeeBot

While VEX U is preparing for competition, the CoffeeBot side is working on a "digital twin." Essentially, before the actual assembly of the robot, our programming team is developing a virtual robot to experiment with the necessary kinematic code to operate it. The virtual model of the robot is run in a web browser, using a JavaScript library called Three.js to render the 3D parts (which are independently simulated).

Community Outreach

11/3 UAA Basketball Halftime

We joined the UAA Basketball Team on the court during halftime as hundreds of 5th graders from around Anchorage watched! Two 5th graders got to compete to score the most disks with our robot, Sid.

11/4 UAA STEM Day

We participated in UAA Stem Day with our robot, Sid. Over 1600 people attended the event, many of whom stopped by our booth to try to score disks in the goal with Sid.
