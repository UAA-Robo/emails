Project Updates

VEX U: Over Under

Last weekend six of our members flew down to West Lafayette, IN to compete as Team ICE1 in the Purdue SIGBots Slam and Jam VexU Qualifier. With 31 competing teams, this was the largest and most competitive VEX U Competition outside of the World Championships. Teams from various universities faced off in matches lasting two minutes, which included a 45-second autonomous period followed by a 1-minute and 15-second driver-controlled period. The primary objective in each match was to accumulate the most points by scoring triballs beneath the goal. The competition was stiff, with over 70% of the teams being of world championship caliber, including the renowned teams BLRS2 and WISCO, who have won the VEX U World Championships multiple times. Despite the challenge, we held their own, ranking 12th in the competition and winning the autonomous period every match but one.

Tree Ring Project

On a side note, a few months ago the club was approached by UAA's Environmental and Natural Resource Institute (ENRI) staff to automate a tree core sampling process. This includes taking high-res microscopic images of the rings in a tree core, and automatically shifting the sample to obtain pictures along the entire sample. The team finished our prototype this past month, and has been fixing bugs, adding features, and finalizing the hardware for the finished product.

Outreach

2/1 Club Showcase
The team participated in UAA's Spring Club Showcase, where we were able to show students from all fields our robots and introduce them to the projects we work on and skills we practice. We staged ourselves on the 4th floor of the Engineering & Industry Building, so that potential members could try driving our competition robots and get a taste of our VEX U project!
